Frequently Asked Questions

How do I export Gemini chat to PDF?

Install Gemini Toolbox and click the "Export Chat" button that appears in your Gemini sidebar. Select the chats you want to export and click "Export to PDF". Your chats will be formatted as clean, professional PDFs with clickable links and proper formatting.

How to delete multiple chats on Gemini at once?

With Gemini Toolbox, click "Bulk Delete" in the sidebar, select multiple chats using checkboxes (or "Select All"), then click "Delete Selected". You can delete dozens or hundreds of chats in seconds instead of one-by-one.

How to add folders to organize Gemini chats?

After installing Gemini Toolbox, click "Manage Folders" in the sidebar. Create custom folders like "Work", "Personal", or "Research", then drag and drop your chats to organize them. You can also search within folders and hide foldered chats from the main view.

How to save and manage prompts in Gemini?

Gemini Toolbox includes a Prompt Library feature. Click "Prompt Library" in the sidebar to save your best prompts, organize them by category (Writing, Research, Coding, etc.), and insert them with one click. Perfect for reusing complex prompts.

Is Gemini Toolbox safe and does it store my data?

Yes, it's completely safe. All processing happens locally in your browser. We never see, store, or transmit your chat content. The extension only runs on gemini.google.com and requires minimal permissions. Your privacy is guaranteed.

How to export all Gemini chats at once?

Gemini Toolbox lets you export multiple chats to PDF in one go. Click "Export Chat" in the sidebar, use "Select All" to choose all your chats, then export them as a single PDF or multiple files. Perfect for backing up your entire chat history.

Can I customize PDF exports from Gemini?

Yes! Gemini Toolbox exports clean, formatted PDFs with proper headings, clickable links, and code blocks preserved. The PDFs are professional enough to share with clients or save for documentation. Each chat maintains its original formatting and structure.
